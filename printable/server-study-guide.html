Our Philosophy: This Is Our Home

We treat every guest like they are coming to our home. That means greeting them warmly, anticipating their needs, and making sure every single visit feels personal and genuine.

One Dream, One Team. The kitchen, the bar, the servers, and the host stand all succeed together. Help your teammates, run food, and always show up ready to work.

The Mallards Standards

  • Full hands in, full hands out — never walk to or from a table empty-handed
  • Anticipate needs — refill drinks before they're empty; bring napkins before they're needed
  • Clean as you go — pre-bus throughout the meal; keep your section spotless
  • Always card — card anyone who appears under 40. No exceptions.
  • Allergy awareness — treat every allergy request as life-or-death serious

The 8 Steps of Service

StepActionKey Points
1Greet within 30 secondsIntroduce yourself; acknowledge the table even if you're busy
2Take drink orders, offer starters"Can I start you with our Walleye Bites or Jerk Chicken Nachos tonight?"
3Deliver drinks, take starter orderAll drinks at once; communicate timing on starters
4Deliver starters, take entrée orderKnow allergens; remind Shrimp Boil is 25–30 min
5Check back within 2 minutes"How is everything tasting?" — address problems immediately
6Maintain the tableRefill drinks, clear plates; full hands in/out every pass
7Suggest dessert, offer carry-out"Can I interest anyone in our Key Lime Pie tonight?"
8Thank & invite backUse their name; "We hope to see you again soon!"

Responsible Alcohol Service

Minnesota Law: You are personally liable under Dram Shop law if you over-serve a guest who then harms themselves or others. Card anyone who appears under 40. No exceptions.

Signs of Intoxication

  • Slurred or slow speech
  • Loss of coordination — stumbling, swaying
  • Glassy or bloodshot eyes
  • Sudden mood change — loud, aggressive, or overly emotional
  • Difficulty placing an order or following conversation

Cutting Off a Guest

  • Be calm and private — step closer, lower your voice
  • "I'm not going to be able to serve you any more alcohol tonight."
  • Offer water, coffee, or food
  • Alert a manager immediately — never handle a cut-off alone

Valid IDs

  • State-issued driver's license or ID (not expired)
  • U.S. Passport or Passport Card
  • Military ID
  • Tribal ID

Check: (1) photo matches, (2) DOB confirms 21+, (3) not expired.
