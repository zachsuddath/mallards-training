Bar Opening & Closing Procedures

Opening (Train on at least 2 shifts)

  • Bar wiped down; mats and grates in place
  • Ice wells filled
  • Dishwasher on — check chemicals, know how to change when empty
  • Sani bucket/towels stocked; change every 2 hours
  • Check beer/wine stock and stock as needed
  • TVs on
  • Fruit tray/mixes/juices/garnish holder out — smell and taste juices for freshness
  • Straws/napkins stocked
  • All syrups prepared
  • Bar top set with menus and silverware — make it inviting

Closing (Train on at least 2 shifts)

  • Stock chilled glassware; pump wine; fill ice; fill bar caddies
  • Plug beer spouts; break down the cold well
  • All sinks cleaned; breakdown soda gun; wash glass rimmer
  • Empty and turn off dish machine; clean grates
  • Bar mats washed and replaced
  • All liquor wells clean and wiped down
  • All bar grates run through the dishwasher
  • Floor swept and mopped
Bar Prep Philosophy: Morning crew does prep and restock. Evening crew does stock and cleaning for morning. Set each other up for success.

Responsible Alcohol Service

MN Dram Shop Law: You are personally liable if you over-serve a guest who then injures themselves or others. No tip, no argument, no shortcut is worth it.

MN Law Highlights

  • Legal drinking age: 21
  • Last call: 2:00 AM — all drinks off tables by 2:30 AM
  • Illegal to serve a visibly intoxicated guest — even if they were served elsewhere first

ID Check — Check 3 Things Every Time

  1. Photo matches the person in front of you
  2. Date of birth confirms they are 21 or older
  3. ID is not expired

Valid IDs: State driver's license or ID, U.S. Passport, Military ID, Tribal ID. Card anyone who appears under 35.

Signs of Intoxication

  • Slurred speech, loss of coordination, glassy eyes
  • Sudden mood change — loud, aggressive, or emotional
  • Difficulty placing an order or following conversation
  • Quick rounds of shots or doubles

How to Cut Off a Guest

  • Be calm, private, and direct
  • "I'm not going to be able to serve you any more alcohol tonight."
  • Offer water, coffee, or food
  • Alert your manager immediately — always
